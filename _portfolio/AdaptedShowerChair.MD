## Introduction

The project was designed to help Miyako, a 9 year old girl with muscular distrophy. Recently, she underwent surgery to fuse her neck and place rods in her back. This improved her posture, but it limited her head movement. Before this project, Miyako was unable to independly shower, relying on her parents to assist her. The goal of the project was to design a shower chair that fit her and gave her enough support to be more independent in her daily activities. Many of the supportive shower chairs that were available on the market were either too small (toddler sized), or they were too big (adult sized). <br><br>

This project was a part of the 2025 Vanderbilt TOM Makeathon Event (10/24/25 - 10/26/25).

## Design

The foundation of this chair was a shower chair purchased off of Amazon ([Link Here](https://www.amazon.com/OasisSpace-Adjustable-Anti-Slip-Disabled-Assembly/dp/B0CYFRMGVQ/ref=sr_1_25_sspa?crid=25PR4CSANL0IU&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.OMCBK0PdtcMTvtuV_IZwG9mVzX32AE2_CQO6FsKyVrlmVlyK2RwG8AN8eEoGZrM0JGs_erNdrOfpFTYqX3ov12hdMclw9shaA4EVGtieqjw.nTSzBMvRVqWZLxZOb9NoJnozkq0NQcoixHEIrWMHuG0&dib_tag=se&keywords=shower%2Bstool%2Bfor%2Binside%2Bshower%2Bkids&qid=1759209954&s=home-garden&sprefix=shower%2Bstool%2Bfor%2Binside%2Bshower%2Bkid%2Cgarden%2C85&sr=1-25-spons&xpid=Z7gV1u5ZDV8U4&sp_csd=d2lkZ2V0TmFtZT1zcF9hdGZfbmV4dA&th=1)). This specific chair allowed the leg height to be adjusted, which was perfect as it would allow the chair to grow with Miyako. <br><br>

To fabricate the supports, it was decided that PVC piping would be ideal. It is sturdy enough to support weight, and it is water resistant. Pool noodles then covered the PVC pipes where Miyako made contact with the back and arm supports to make the the seat more comfortable. <br><br>

Finally, a shower bag was attached to one of the arms to allow Miyako to more easily access her soap and other shower tools. To do this, small hooks were attached to the arm ([Link Here](https://www.amazon.com/PCS-Hanger-Connector-Hooks-Organizers/dp/B0DDT1FJM1/ref=sr_1_7?crid=EGX2SHB9MU3D&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.tDkr9xSCN4z3rqMqE8GvINomXKdOVTflkpLybpTTiQoy2kKqEfA-Y3nO9OT4StNMuUHo71GaqwY5iz-rxxoymEQGELA0VJvUy5uol0NWQbGwZjR2GP-1-AIYWeuuwK-_Yg98KJsDH8MZrqZlIXlKbVtvIItk1nW5PGX7gScsfzQhaSeUS4FLCk825XmPysZWef0C8KpDX-cuOHvFTYOfUp9XWTBqBfxQQh32WWYQVHMvYBsR-MuvsplkNJOga-NmgPeTFV9X--pYUc6DlOmA2RG6s_iHLZsj5N-hW4yZXM8.JcjtOp4Q_r5Qtr1Tpcg1C0rV1GxZayE8lJUrKvlCfok&dib_tag=se&keywords=hanger%2Bhooks%2Bplastic&qid=1759210949&s=hi&sprefix=hanger%2Bhooks%2Bplastic%2Ctools%2C81&sr=1-7&th=1)), and the bag was hooked onto it to allow the bag to be taken off and washed periodically.
